Section V: Glossary of Terms

A. Financial Metrics & Terminology

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The value of contracted recurring revenue normalized to a one-year period, critical for SaaS and subscription businesses.

Burn Rate: The rate at which a company consumes cash reserves, typically measured monthly. High burn (4-5/5) indicates rapid cash consumption requiring careful monitoring.

CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Total sales and marketing expenses divided by number of new customers acquired. Industry benchmark for fintech: $10K-50K for enterprise clients.

Carried Interest (Carry): A share of profits paid to investment managers beyond their initial investment, typically 20% of profits above a hurdle rate. Core service offering for EWM Global (30% of revenue).

Cash Runway: The number of months a company can operate with current cash reserves at current burn rate. Standard requirement: 12-18 months for growth companies.

COSO Framework: Committee of Sponsoring Organizations framework for enterprise risk management, widely adopted standard for internal controls.

Current Ratio: Current assets divided by current liabilities. Measure of liquidity. Healthy threshold: 1.5 or higher.

Deferred Compensation: Portion of employee compensation paid at a later date, often for tax advantages. Primary service line for EWM Global (69% of revenue).

EBITDA Margin: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization as percentage of revenue. Fintech benchmark: 15-25%.

Gross Margin: (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue. For professional services: 50-80% expected range.

LTV (Customer Lifetime Value): Total revenue expected from a customer relationship. Calculation: Average Revenue per Account × Gross Margin × Customer Lifetime.

LTV:CAC Ratio: Customer Lifetime Value divided by Customer Acquisition Cost. Minimum viable: 3:1, excellent: >5:1.

Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers including expansions. Best-in-class: >120%.

Rule of 40: Growth rate + profit margin should exceed 40% for SaaS companies. Indicator of balanced growth and profitability.

Unit Economics: Revenue and costs at individual transaction/customer level. Positive unit economics essential for scalability.

Working Capital: Current Assets - Current Liabilities. Measure of operational liquidity and efficiency.

B. Operational & Technology Terms

API (Application Programming Interface): Software intermediary allowing applications to communicate. Critical for fintech integrations.

AIOps: Artificial Intelligence for IT Operations. Uses machine learning to automate IT problem resolution.

Blue Ocean Strategy: Creating uncontested market space rather than competing in existing markets. Framework for differentiation.

Business Process Automation (BPA): Technology-enabled automation of complex business processes. Target: 70%+ for routine processes.

CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment): Development practice enabling frequent, automated software releases. Best practice: Daily deployments.

Customer Effort Score (CES): Measure of ease of customer interaction. Scale 1-5, target: 4+.

Digital Transformation: Integration of digital technology fundamentally changing operations and value delivery.

Infrastructure as Code (IaC): Managing IT infrastructure through machine-readable definition files. Enables instant scalability.

Kaizen: Japanese philosophy of continuous improvement through small, incremental changes.

Lean Principles: Systematic method for waste minimization without sacrificing productivity. Core principles: Value, Value Stream, Flow, Pull, Perfection.

MEDDPICC: Enterprise sales methodology (Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition).

Net Promoter Score (NPS): Customer loyalty metric. Calculation: % Promoters - % Detractors. Excellent: >50.

OKRs (Objectives and Key Results): Goal-setting framework linking company, team, and personal objectives to measurable results.

RACI Matrix: Responsibility assignment matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) clarifying decision rights.

RPA (Robotic Process Automation): Software robots automating repetitive, rules-based tasks. ROI typically 200-300% year one.

SaaS (Software as a Service): Software distribution model with centralized hosting and subscription access.

SIEM (Security Information and Event Management): Real-time analysis of security alerts. Critical for fintech cybersecurity.

Six Sigma: Quality management methodology targeting 3.4 defects per million opportunities. Uses DMAIC framework.

SLA (Service Level Agreement): Commitment between service provider and client defining service standards.

Straight-Through Processing (STP): Automated end-to-end processing without manual intervention. Target: 85%+ for standard transactions.

Value Stream Mapping: Lean technique analyzing flow of materials/information to deliver products/services.

Zero Trust Architecture: Security model requiring verification for every person/device accessing resources.

C. Human Resources & Leadership Terms

360-Degree Feedback: Performance evaluation incorporating input from supervisors, peers, subordinates, and self.

Advisory Board: External advisors providing strategic guidance without fiduciary responsibility of board directors.

Bench Strength: Depth of talent ready to fill key positions. Measured by succession readiness percentage.

Change Management: Structured approach to transitioning individuals, teams, and organizations to desired future state.

Culture Score: Composite measure of organizational culture health. Components: values alignment, engagement, psychological safety.

Employee Net Promoter Score (eNPS): Likelihood employees would recommend company as place to work. Good: >20, Excellent: >50.

Engagement Score: Measure of employee emotional commitment and discretionary effort. Target: 4+/5 or 80%+.

HRIS (Human Resources Information System): Software managing core HR processes and employee data.

Knowledge Transfer: Systematic approach to sharing critical information and expertise, especially during transitions.

Leadership Pipeline: Framework developing leaders at every level. Book by Charan, Drotter, and Noel (2001).

Onboarding: Process integrating new employees. Best practice: Structured 90-day program with clear milestones.

Organizational Effectiveness: Measure of how well organization achieves objectives with minimum resources.

Performance Management: Continuous process of setting objectives, assessing progress, and providing feedback.

Psychological Safety: Team climate where members feel safe to take risks and make mistakes. Critical for innovation.

RAPID Framework: Decision-making model from Bain & Company (Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide).

Span of Control: Number of direct reports per manager. Modern organizations: 7-10 for knowledge workers.

Succession Planning: Process identifying and developing potential future leaders. Best practice: 2+ candidates per critical role.

Talent Density: Concentration of high-performers in organization. Netflix principle: "Adequate performance gets generous severance."

Time to Productivity: Period for new employee to reach full performance. Financial services average: 90-120 days.

Turnover Rate: Percentage of employees leaving annually. Fintech average: 15-18%, concerning: >20%.

D. Sales & Marketing Terms

Account-Based Marketing (ABM): B2B strategy treating individual accounts as markets of one. Critical for enterprise sales.

Attribution Modeling: Determining which marketing touchpoints receive credit for conversion.

B2B (Business-to-Business): Commerce transactions between businesses. Characteristics: Longer sales cycles, multiple stakeholders.

BANT: Sales qualification framework (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline). Traditional but being replaced by modern frameworks.

Churn Rate: Percentage of customers stopping service in given period. Annual B2B benchmark: 10-15%.

Conversion Rate: Percentage of prospects becoming customers. B2B software average: 22%.

Customer Success: Proactive approach ensuring customers achieve desired outcomes. Evolution from reactive support.

Demand Generation: Creating awareness and interest in products/services. Encompasses lead generation and nurturing.

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): Description of company that would gain most value from product/service.

Lead Scoring: Ranking prospects based on likelihood to convert. Best practice: Behavioral + demographic scoring.

Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL): Prospect deemed ready for sales engagement based on engagement criteria.

Pipeline Coverage Ratio: Pipeline value divided by revenue target. Recommended: 3-4x for quarterly targets.

Product-Led Growth (PLG): Business methodology where product usage drives customer acquisition and expansion.

Sales Accepted Lead (SAL): MQL that sales team agrees to pursue. Acceptance rate benchmark: 70%+.

Sales Cycle: Time from initial contact to closed deal. Enterprise B2B average: 84-120 days.

Sales Velocity: Rate of revenue generation. Calculation: (Opportunities × Win Rate × Average Deal Size) / Sales Cycle Length.

SDR (Sales Development Representative): Role focused on qualifying leads and setting appointments for account executives.

TAM (Total Addressable Market): Total revenue opportunity available for product/service.

Value Engineering: Process quantifying customer value creation. Critical for enterprise deal justification.

Win Rate: Percentage of qualified opportunities resulting in closed deals. B2B benchmark: 20-30%.

E. Compliance & Risk Management Terms

AML (Anti-Money Laundering): Regulations requiring financial institutions to monitor and report suspicious activities.

Basel III: International regulatory framework for banks covering capital adequacy, stress testing, and liquidity.

Business Continuity Planning (BCP): Creating systems of prevention and recovery from potential threats.

Compliance Risk: Risk of legal/regulatory sanctions, financial loss, or reputation damage from non-compliance.

ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act): Federal law setting standards for retirement and health plans.

FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority): Self-regulatory organization overseeing broker-dealers.

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation): EU regulation on data protection and privacy.

Information Security Management System (ISMS): Framework of policies and procedures for managing information risks.

Internal Controls: Processes designed to provide reasonable assurance regarding objective achievement.

KRI (Key Risk Indicator): Metric providing early signal of increasing risk exposure.

KYC (Know Your Customer): Process of verifying client identity and assessing suitability and risks.

NIST Cybersecurity Framework: Voluntary framework of standards and best practices for cybersecurity risk management.

Operational Risk: Risk of loss from inadequate/failed internal processes, people, systems, or external events.

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard): Security standards for organizations handling credit cards.

Recovery Time Objective (RTO): Maximum acceptable downtime after incident before business operations must resume.

Regulatory Capital: Minimum capital regulators require financial institutions to hold.

Risk Appetite: Level and type of risk organization willing to accept in pursuit of objectives.

Risk Heat Map: Visual representation of risks plotted by likelihood and impact.

Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX): US federal law mandating practices in financial record keeping and reporting.

Section 409A: IRS code governing nonqualified deferred compensation. Critical for EWM Global's services.

SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2): Auditing procedure ensuring service providers securely manage data.

Three Lines of Defense: Risk management model (1: Operational management, 2: Risk management/compliance, 3: Internal audit).
